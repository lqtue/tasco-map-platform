Job Purpose

The Map Operations Specialist owns the quality of the digital map system that powers our navigation ecosystem. This role sets data-quality standards for OpenStreetMap (OSM), leads digitization of Vietnam's traffic-sign network, and tunes the routing engine to deliver accurate just-in-time alerts for millions of trips per day.

This is a high-impact role that works across Engineering, Data Science, and Product to ensure the navigation experience meets international standards.

How the Role Works — Data to Navigation Quality

2

Key Responsibilities

A. Map Data Quality Governance (OpenStreetMap)
  • Own and define data-quality standards for OpenStreetMap (OSM) data across all operating regions.
  • Design and maintain large-scale data monitoring, cleaning, and QA pipelines.
  • Resolve issues in road-network topology, node connectivity, and missing/incorrect tags to keep data routing-ready for Valhalla, GraphHopper, and OSRM.
  • Define and track data-quality KPIs by region and road class.
B. Traffic Signs & Restrictions Management
  • Lead digitization and continuous update of Vietnam's traffic-sign network: speed limits, enforcement cameras, turn/parking/one-way restrictions, lane info, and warning signs.
  • Manage complex traffic rules: turn restrictions, time-based restrictions, and road closures (segment / route / zone).
  • Ensure sign data is accurately map-matched into the routing graph to prevent routing into prohibited roads.
  • Establish data-collection workflows (cameras, dashcams, GPS probes, user reports) and a continuous update pipeline.
C. Just-in-time Alerts & Navigation Experience
  • Build logic and manage spatial data so the system emits accurate real-time alerts (speed limits, cameras, route changes) as vehicles move.
  • Partner with Engineering to optimize map-matching (HMM, snap-to-road), minimizing latency and false positives / false negatives.
  • Define SLOs/SLAs for alert accuracy and latency; set up monitoring dashboards.
D. Troubleshooting, RCA & Routing Tuning
  • Lead resolution of critical positioning/navigation incidents from systems and users (GPS drift, on-ground sign mismatches).
  • Conduct root-cause analysis (RCA) for trips with wrong ETA or suboptimal routes.
  • Tune routing-engine costing models, penalties, and restrictions based on real-world data.
  • Translate insights into actionable improvements for product and data infrastructure.
E. Technical Leadership & Mentorship
  • Mentor map operators and junior engineers; review work and raise the team's quality bar.
  • Build documentation, playbooks, and standard operating procedures (SOPs) for map-data work.
  • Partner with Product, Engineering, and Data Science to shape the map-quality roadmap.
3

Requirements

Must-Have

  • Minimum 3 years of hands-on experience as a Map Operator, GIS Data Engineer, or equivalent at a maps, navigation, ride-hailing, or logistics company.
  • Bachelor's degree in Geomatics, GIS, Computer Science, or a related field.
  • Deep knowledge of Vietnam's road-traffic sign system and OpenStreetMap data structures (tags, relations, restrictions).
  • Proficient in QGIS, PostgreSQL/PostGIS, and spatial data analysis tooling.
  • Solid understanding of routing algorithms and at least one open-source routing engine (Valhalla, GraphHopper, or OSRM).
  • Proficient in Python for ETL, automation, and internal tooling.
  • Experience with map editors (JOSM, iD, or RapID) and the Overpass API.
  • Strong English reading comprehension for technical documentation.

Nice-to-Have

  • Experience with map-matching algorithms (HMM, snap-to-road) and large-scale GPS-probe data processing.
  • Familiarity with osm2pgsql, Osmium, or planet-scale OSM tooling.
  • Experience with streaming/real-time systems (Kafka, Flink) for location data.
  • Contributions to the OpenStreetMap community or related open-source projects.
  • Background in Automotive / In-car Navigation / ADAS.

Soft Skills & Mindset

  • Data-driven mindset; decisions grounded in metrics.
  • Strong communication with both technical and non-technical stakeholders.
  • Able to balance data quality, delivery speed, and operating cost.
  • Takes ownership proactively; works independently and can lead small working groups when needed.
4

Success Metrics — 6 to 12 Months

Measurable improvement in OSM data-quality KPIs in priority regions (targets agreed with manager).
Reduce false-positive / false-negative rates of just-in-time alerts to target levels.
Reduce incidents of routing into restricted or wrong roads.
Deliver a scalable sign-digitization pipeline ready for new-city expansion.
Build and roll out SOPs across the team.
